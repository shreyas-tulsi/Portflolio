January, 2020

TicTacToe.py was a game I created, in which a user could play a fully-functional game of TicTacToe against a computer opponent. To create the game board, I assigned each portion of the board to a certain index, making it simple to replace the white space, with an 'X' or a 'O'. Scratch Project

October 2019(CSP CLASS)
Head Soccer- Collaboration with Mason Valentine

If I made Head Soccer just a one player-game with a constant opponent, people would have eventually gotten bored of the game and stop playing. Due to this reason, I needed to find a way in which I could keep increasing the difficulty(essentially increasing the skill of the opposition). The way I did this was by controlling the reactiveness of the C.P.U concerning the player's movements. As you completed a series of levels, the C.P.U's delay would be reduced everytime by 500 milliseconds. This way, the player constantly felt challenged and experienced a true sense of accomplishment when they finished. An extremely important lesson I learnt was how my program to appeal to an audience, and keep them attracted.
